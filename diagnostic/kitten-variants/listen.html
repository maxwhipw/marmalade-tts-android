What we’re choosing between

Your stated policy: default to highest-quant unless lower-quant is validated. Strict reading says nano-fp32 is the only pure-fp32 option. But upstream mini ships with a hand-picked mixed-precision quantization (fp32 + fp16 + selective int8/uint8), which is materially different from the blanket dynamic int8 that sinks nano-int8. Mixed-precision is a quantize-aware design choice, not a careless squeeze.

Pick what passes your ear. After you decide:

  • If mini sounds good → mini is recommended default, nano-fp32 + micro are opt-in alternatives.
  • If mini sounds bad too → nano-fp32 is the only validated option; mini ships labelled experimental or doesn’t ship at all.
  • If micro wins (40M params, smaller than mini) → ship that as default.
